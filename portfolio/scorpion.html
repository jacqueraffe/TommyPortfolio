Starting out simple

MiniMe

Five concepts to transport plastic balls, followed by a decision matrix to evaluate the options and select the best. I translated a cardboard prototype to a manufacturable design from bolted sheet metal and aluminum L-brackets.

With the addition of the front claw, the MiniMe encountered issues climbing the 25-degree incline, which was necessary for later challenges. I evaluated how battery placement affects the center of mass and how the coefficient of friction and normal force impact wheel traction to ensure no-slip conditions while climbing the ramp.

Most Critical Module (MCM)

The Claw

Design a robot to interact with the Gooble Box, a 2x2 button panel on a 25° incline. The robot must start from a colored zone above or below the ramp, press buttons in an alternating sequence, apply specific forces, and maximize points in 90 seconds.

I designed a unique 4-bar linkage claw mechanism optimized for picking up scoring objects with high precision and speed. To push the buttons, I developed a camshaft design that converts the rotational motion of a motor into the linear motion of shafts. The rotationally offset cams ensure the buttons are pressed at a constant frequency.

Chassis and Drivetrain

Key Idea: By making a narrow wheelbase, the robot can drive in the space between the buttons. Furthermore, to avoid bottoming out when climbing onto the panel, continuous contact with the ground is maintained with tank treads.

This design uses a 24-inch timing belt as the tank treads, alongside rods, LDO motors, bushings, e-clips, washers, and custom acrylic timing gears. Special consideration was given to the minimum number of teeth in contact with the gear to ensure adequate belt tooth shear strength.
