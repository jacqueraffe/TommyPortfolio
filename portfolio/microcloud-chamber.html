microCloud Chamber

Research / Physics

Project Overview

The microCloud Chamber was a research project designed to make particle physics accessible. Traditional cloud chambers are large, expensive, and require dry ice. I engineered a miniaturized, Peltier-cooled chamber capable of detecting ionizing radiation in a compact form factor.

How it Works

The device uses a multi-stage Peltier cooling system to create a supersaturated alcohol vapor environment. When a charged particle (like an alpha or beta particle) passes through the vapor, it ionizes the air molecules, causing the alcohol to condense into visible droplet trails.
