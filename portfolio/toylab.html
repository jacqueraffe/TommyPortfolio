Toy Product Design

MIT Toy Lab (2.00B) is a freshman-year product design capstone class that teaches a variety of mechanical engineering skills through projects. Below is a collection of some of my favorites!

Toy Product Design
Collection of Projects

Year
Spring 2023

  • Polaroid Camera: CAD

    Given a frame and hard dimensional constraints, I designed a visually appealing shell and programmed an Arduino microcontroller for a game product (“t00bers”) in the form of a Polaroid camera.

  • Dr. Time: Team Project

    Collaborated with a team of six to design and manufacture a toy product from ideation to presentation. I focused on CAD prototypes and Arduino C++ programming. Our toy, Dr. Time, is a fast-paced pattern recognition game.

  • Cat Push Toy: Woodworking

    Designed and manufactured a childen’s push toy with careful consideration for affordances and safety. Utilized bandsaws, belt and disk sanders, and wood glue and polish.

  • Vector Vortex: Sketching

    One of many sketches of game product ideas early in the semester. Overall, I produced about a dozen sketches all loosely focused around the theme “Timeless.”
